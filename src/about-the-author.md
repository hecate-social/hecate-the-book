# About the Author

Raf Lefever is a software architect and engineer based in Leuven, Belgium. He has been building software systems since 1990, starting on mainframes and working his way through every paradigm shift the industry has offered -- and a few it tried to forget.

His career spans nearly every layer of the enterprise stack. He built one of the early SaaS platforms for inter-business process management at IQ-Probe in the late 1990s, when "SaaS" wasn't a word yet and "the cloud" was still something you looked at through a window. He led a digital transformation from OpenBasic to .NET at Habufa, a Dutch furniture wholesaler, back when "digital transformation" meant convincing people that Delphi and Interbase weren't going to last forever. He architected control room software for public transportation at Prodata Mobility, managing near- and offshore teams across India and Romania. At Q-Park, he optimized parking management processes across 5,000 European facilities using BPMN and C4 modeling, and contributed to a research project on decentralized, low-energy parking systems with the University of Maastricht.

From 2017 onward, his focus shifted to the BEAM ecosystem and event-driven architecture. At Discomco, he contributed to ReckonDB -- a BEAM-native event store built on Khepri and Ra -- and its surrounding libraries: the gater API, client SDKs in .NET, Go, and Node.js, and the evoq CQRS framework. He designed edge components for an agricultural IoT platform that aggregated data streams from dairy farm robots, with zero-user-interaction deployment and automatic software updates. He trained engineers in event sourcing and CQRS, facilitated Event Storming and DDD workshops for maritime logistics, authored cybersecurity documentation for bulk sea-to-rail projects, and architected systems for the European Commission and a global industrial printing platform with over 2,000 field installations.

The work described in this book -- Hecate, Macula Mesh, Martha Studio, the Venture lifecycle, TWEANN on the BEAM -- is the synthesis of all of it. Thirty-five years of watching the industry oscillate between centralization and decentralization, between thick clients and thin clients and thick clients again, between "the database is the source of truth" and "maybe we should stop overwriting things." Every dead end taught something. Every hype cycle left behind one or two ideas worth keeping.

The Autonomous Stack was not designed in a vacuum. It was built iteratively, one vertical slice at a time, on a cluster of four Celeron mini-PCs drawing less power than the mainframe cooling systems where this journey began. The event store, the masterless mesh, the neuroevolution engine, the plugin architecture -- each piece exists because a real problem demanded it, not because a conference talk made it sound exciting.

Much of this book was written in collaboration with AI. Not as a novelty, but as a practical demonstration of the thesis: that human judgment and machine capability, working together with clear boundaries, produce better results than either alone. The code in these pages runs. The architecture holds load. The system evolves. That's what matters.

Raf holds a BSc in Engineering Sciences from KU Leuven, certifications in Erlang/OTP, Elixir with Phoenix, Golang, .NET Framework, and Radio Electronics (HAREC). His current reading list includes genetic algorithms, neuroevolution through Erlang, and autonomous systems -- which, given this book, should surprise no one.
